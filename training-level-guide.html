How to Choose Between Varsity and JV Workouts
Based on Jack Daniels' Running Formula

NO → Follow JV workouts
YES → Continue to Question 2

NO → Follow JV workouts
YES → Continue to Question 3

NO → Follow JV workouts (build gradually)
YES → Continue to Question 4

👦 BOYS 5K STANDARDS

Time-Based Guidelines:

Strong Varsity: Under 17:30
Varsity: 17:30 - 19:30
Varsity/JV Border: 19:30 - 21:00
JV: 21:00 - 24:00
Developmental: Over 24:00

👧 GIRLS 5K STANDARDS

Time-Based Guidelines:

Strong Varsity: Under 20:30
Varsity: 20:30 - 22:30
Varsity/JV Border: 22:30 - 24:00
JV: 24:00 - 27:00
Developmental: Over 27:00

📋 JACK DANIELS' KEY TRAINING PRINCIPLES

Principle #1: Train at Your Current Fitness Level
Your body adapts best when training matches your current ability, not your goal ability. Be honest about where you are today.
Principle #2: Consistency Beats Intensity
It's better to complete 100% of JV workouts than to struggle through 70% of varsity workouts. Missed or incomplete workouts don't build fitness.
Principle #3: The 2-3 Week Rule
Training adaptations take 2-3 weeks to manifest. If you've been handling JV workouts well for 3+ weeks, consider moving up.
Principle #4: Quality Over Quantity
Running the correct paces for shorter distances is better than running too fast/slow for longer distances. Respect the workout's purpose.

⚠️ RED FLAGS - Switch to JV Workouts If You Experience:

  • ✗ Extreme fatigue lasting more than 24 hours after workouts
  • ✗ Unable to hit prescribed paces in workouts
  • ✗ Declining race performances despite hard training
  • ✗ Persistent soreness or nagging injuries
  • ✗ Dreading practice or losing motivation
  • ✗ Sleep problems or getting sick frequently

✅ GREEN FLAGS - Ready for Varsity Workouts:

  • ✓ Completing current workouts with energy left over
  • ✓ Recovering well between hard days
  • ✓ Hitting all prescribed paces comfortably
  • ✓ Excited and motivated for practice
  • ✓ Sleeping well and feeling healthy
  • ✓ Recent race times in varsity range

🔄 BORDERLINE ATHLETES (Varsity/JV Border Times)

If you're in the border zone, consider these factors:

  • Training Age: How many seasons have you been running?
    • First year? → Lean toward JV workouts
    • 2+ years? → Consider varsity if recovering well
  • Summer Base: Did you run consistently over summer?
    • Yes (25+ miles/week) → Try varsity workouts
    • No or minimal → Start with JV workouts
  • Growth Status: Are you still growing rapidly?
    • Yes → JV workouts (your body needs energy for growth)
    • No → Varsity workouts if other factors align
🎯 THE TWO-WEEK TEST

Still unsure? Try this approach:

  1. Start with JV workouts for 2 weeks
  2. If you feel strong and recovered after every workout, move to varsity
  3. If varsity feels too hard after 2 weeks, return to JV without shame
  4. Re-evaluate every 3-4 weeks as your fitness improves

Remember: You can always move up OR down based on how you feel. This isn't a permanent decision!

💪 REMEMBER THE GOAL

The purpose of training is to improve and stay healthy, not to survive the hardest possible workouts.

Choose the level that allows you to:

✓ Complete every workout successfully
✓ Feel motivated and energized
✓ Race your best when it matters
✓ Enjoy the journey!

"Train smarter, not just harder!"
