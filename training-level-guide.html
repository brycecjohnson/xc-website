üìã JACK DANIELS' KEY TRAINING PRINCIPLES

Principle #1: Train at Your Current Fitness Level
Your body adapts best when training matches your current ability, not your goal ability. Be honest about where you are today.
Principle #2: Consistency Beats Intensity
It's better to complete 100% of JV workouts than to struggle through 70% of varsity workouts. Missed or incomplete workouts don't build fitness.
Principle #3: The 2-3 Week Rule
Training adaptations take 2-3 weeks to manifest. If you've been handling JV workouts well for 3+ weeks, consider moving up.
Principle #4: Quality Over Quantity
Running the correct paces for shorter distances is better than running too fast/slow for longer distances. Respect the workout's purpose.

üîÑ BORDERLINE ATHLETES (Varsity/JV Border Times)

If you're in the border zone, consider these factors:

  • Training Age: How many seasons have you been running?
    • First year? ‚Üí Lean toward JV workouts
    • 2+ years? ‚Üí Consider varsity if recovering well
  • Summer Base: Did you run consistently over summer?
    • Yes (25+ miles/week) ‚Üí Try varsity workouts
    • No or minimal ‚Üí Start with JV workouts
  • Growth Status: Are you still growing rapidly?
    • Yes ‚Üí JV workouts (your body needs energy for growth)
    • No ‚Üí Varsity workouts if other factors align
üéØ THE TWO-WEEK TEST

Still unsure? Try this approach:

  1. Start with JV workouts for 2 weeks
  2. If you feel strong and recovered after every workout, move to varsity
  3. If varsity feels too hard after 2 weeks, return to JV without shame
  4. Re-evaluate every 3-4 weeks as your fitness improves

Remember: You can always move up OR down based on how you feel. This isn't a permanent decision!
