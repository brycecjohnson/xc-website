Hawks, we're heading into another important league meet this Friday at Estes Park! The Longs Peak Invite is a great opportunity to test ourselves on a challenging course and continue building momentum for the championship meets ahead.

Tuesday's interval workout is designed to maintain your race sharpness. Hit your 800s at goal 5K pace - no faster! This workout should feel controlled and smooth. We're in the peak phase of training, so these intervals are about maintaining speed, not building it. Wednesday's recovery run is crucial - keep it truly easy. Thursday's shakeout with strides will have you ready to race.

The Estes Park course is beautiful but challenging. The two-loop format means you'll know exactly what's coming on lap two. Use lap one to establish position and rhythm, then attack on lap two when others are fading. The altitude (7,500 feet) will be a factor - start conservatively and build into your race.

Remember our season-long focus on the Jack Daniels methodology - you've built your aerobic base (E runs), raised your lactate threshold (T workouts), improved your VO2 max (I sessions), and sharpened your speed (R work). Trust your training! You're ready to run your best race of the season. Let's show everyone what months of smart, consistent training can produce!

üèÉ HEALTH TIP OF THE WEEK

Racing at Altitude

The Estes Park course sits at 7,500 feet elevation - significantly higher than our normal training grounds. This week's preparation focuses on being ready for the altitude challenge:

Sleep: Aim for 8+ hours Wednesday and Thursday nights. Poor sleep two nights before a race impacts performance more than the night before.

Hydration: Start hydrating NOW. Drink water consistently throughout the week, not just Thursday. Add a pinch of salt to your water bottles for better absorption.

Nutrition: Wednesday and Thursday, focus on quality carbs (pasta, rice, oatmeal) but don't overdo it. Eat familiar foods - no experiments! Friday's pre-race meal should be 3-4 hours before (eat lunch early on the bus).

Mental prep: Visualize the two-loop course and your pacing strategy. Remember that everyone will feel the altitude - stay calm when breathing feels harder than usual. Focus on rhythm and relaxation!
