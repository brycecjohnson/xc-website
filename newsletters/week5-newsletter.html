üí¨ A NOTE FROM YOUR COACHES

Race week is here! This will be an important test of our training so far. This week's training is designed to sharpen your fitness while keeping you fresh for Saturday. Tuesday's interval workout targets VO‚ÇÇ max - these should feel hard but sustainable. Remember: you should be able to run the last rep as fast as the first.

Thursday's cruise intervals at threshold pace serve as your final quality workout before the race. These mile repeats should feel "comfortably hard" - about your 10K race pace. This workout primes your lactate threshold system without creating excessive fatigue.

Friday is all about staying loose and relaxed. The easy miles and strides help maintain your neuromuscular coordination without tiring you out. Get to bed early Friday night - aim for at least 8 hours of sleep!

For Saturday's race, remember our three keys: 1) Start controlled - don't get caught up in the early chaos, 2) Work the middle mile - this is where races are won, 3) Empty the tank in the final 800m. Trust your training and race with confidence!

üèÉ HEALTH TIP OF THE WEEK

Race Week Nutrition Strategy

During race week, maintain your normal eating habits Monday through Wednesday. Starting Thursday, slightly increase your carbohydrate intake - add an extra serving of rice, pasta, or sweet potatoes at meals. Friday's dinner should be familiar foods that you know digest well - avoid trying anything new!

Saturday morning, eat 2-3 hours before your race. Good options include oatmeal with banana, toast with peanut butter and honey, or a bagel with light cream cheese. Sip water throughout the morning but stop heavy drinking 45 minutes before your race. Bring a small snack (granola bar, banana) for after you warm up.
