üèÉ TRAINING TIP OF THE WEEK

Managing a Hard Training Week

This week's schedule is intentionally challenging - here's how to handle it successfully:

Respect the Easy Days: Monday and Thursday are truly easy. Running too hard on recovery days will compromise your hard workouts and increase injury risk. If you can't hold a conversation, you're going too fast.

Nail Your Paces: Don't run Tuesday's intervals faster than 5K pace or Friday's reps faster than mile pace. The prescribed paces provide optimal training stimulus. Running faster just leaves you tired without additional benefit.

Fuel Properly: Hard training requires adequate nutrition. Don't skip meals or drastically change your diet during Fall Break. Carbohydrates are your friend this week.

Sleep is Training: Your body repairs and adapts during sleep. Aim for 8-9 hours nightly, especially after hard workout days. Late nights hurt performance more than you realize.

Listen to Your Body: Hard week doesn't mean injury-risking week. If something hurts (not tired, but actually hurts), be smart. Contact coaches via TeamSnap immediately if you have pain concerns.
