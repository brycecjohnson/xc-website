üèÉ TRAINING TIP OF THE WEEK

Self-Directed Training During Fall Break

Training on your own requires extra discipline and smart planning. Here's how to make the most of Fall Break training:

Schedule It: Treat your runs like appointments. Set a specific time each day and stick to it. Morning runs often work best during break - get it done before the day gets away from you.

Find a Partner: Text your teammates tonight to set up training times. Even meeting for 2-3 workouts makes a huge difference in motivation and performance.

Location Variety: Use Fall Break to explore new running routes. This mental freshness can make solo runs more enjoyable. Just make sure to measure distances accurately.

Pace Discipline: Without coaches watching, it's tempting to run too fast on easy days or too slow on workout days. Stick to the prescribed paces - they're designed for optimal Regionals preparation.

Recovery Matters: With no structured practice schedule, don't forget recovery. Easy means EASY. Your body adapts during rest, not just during hard work.
