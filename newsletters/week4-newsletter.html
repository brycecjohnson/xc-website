üí¨ A NOTE FROM YOUR COACHES

This week marks the beginning of Phase II in our training plan. We're introducing quality speed work while maintaining our aerobic base. Tuesday's 400m repeats focus on running economy and form - these should feel fast but controlled, not all-out sprints. Focus on smooth, efficient running.

Thursday's 20-minute threshold run is a key workout in the Jack Daniels system. This "comfortably hard" effort teaches your body to clear lactate efficiently. Find a pace you could hold for about an hour in a race - challenging but controlled.

For Monday's Labor Day run, even though there's no formal practice, it's important to get your easy miles in. This recovery run sets you up for Tuesday's speed work. Run with a teammate if possible!

üèÉ HEALTH TIP OF THE WEEK

Post-Workout Recovery Window

You have a 30-minute window after hard workouts when your muscles are primed to absorb nutrients. Aim for a 3:1 or 4:1 ratio of carbohydrates to protein. Simple options include chocolate milk, a banana with peanut butter, or a recovery drink. This speeds recovery and prepares you for the next day's training.
